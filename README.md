# Archive of Genetics (AOG)

Archive of Genetics (AOG) is a simple, open genetics archive where anyone can explore and contribute information about genes.

The goal is to make genetics easier to understand and more accessible. Instead of overwhelming users with highly technical language, AOG focuses on clear, readable descriptions and a structure that makes browsing and searching straightforward.

This is a data-driven website, not an AI-powered app. There are no AI features in the platform itself.

---

## Story

I created Archive of Genetics because I kept running into the same problem.

When I look at online databases like NCBI Gene, Ensembl, or UniProt, I feel completely lost. The information is powerful, but it’s written in a way that feels like you need a PhD just to understand the basics. There are long technical descriptions, complex terminology, and interfaces that aren’t really designed for everyday people.

And that bothered me.

Genetics isn’t just for researchers in labs. It’s about all of us. It explains traits, diseases, inheritance—basically how life works at a fundamental level. So why is it so hard to access and understand?

Archive of Genetics (AOG) is my attempt to fix that.

The idea is simple:
- make genetic information easier to read  
- make it searchable and structured  
- let people contribute and expand it  

No heavy jargon. No gatekeeping. Just clear, accessible information.

I built this as a solo project, as a student, using AI Studio mainly to speed up development and prototyping. Even though it was built with AI tools, the website itself doesn’t rely on AI features.
